3 Days in Hanoi: A Complete Itinerary (2026)

📅 April 15, 2026 · ☕ 7 min read · ✍️ By MiaCasa Team
Hoàn Kiếm Lake at sunset — the perfect way to end a day in Hanoi

Planning 3 days in Hanoi? This itinerary helps you experience the city beyond the usual tourist checklist—with local food, quieter neighborhoods, and places that make Hanoi memorable.

Day 1: Old Quarter, Street Food, and First Impressions

Start your trip in the heart of the chaotic, colorful historic center of Hanoi—the Old Quarter.

Morning

  • Begin at Hoàn Kiếm Lake (Lake of the Restored Sword) before the crowds arrive
  • Walk across the red Thê Húc Bridge to Ngọc Sơn Temple
  • Then wander into the maze of 36 ancient streets, each named after the trade once practiced there

Afternoon

  • Continue exploring the Old Quarter's narrow alleyways
  • Sit at a sidewalk café and watch daily life unfold—scooters, street vendors, and local conversations
  • Visit Đồng Xuân Market for a glimpse of local commerce

What to Eat Today

  • Phở for breakfast — find a busy street stall, not a restaurant
  • Bánh mì for lunch — the perfect sandwich
  • Bún chả for dinner — grilled pork with noodles (what Obama ate with Bourdain)

Evening

Walk through the night market (weekends only) or find a rooftop café with lake views. The Old Quarter glows at night.

Day 2: Culture, Cafés, and Train Street

Slow things down on your second day. This is when Hanoi starts to feel real.

Morning: Văn Miếu Area

  • Visit Văn Miếu – Quốc Tử Giám (Temple of Literature), Vietnam's first university — one of Hanoi's most peaceful sites
  • Afterward, walk through the surrounding streets. This area feels calmer, more residential, and genuinely local
  • Stop at a small café that doesn't cater to tourists

💡 Tip: MiaCasa Hanoi is located in this neighborhood — you can see what local life actually looks like.

Afternoon: Train Street

Head to one of Hanoi's most unique attractions — Train Street, where trains pass just inches from homes and cafés.

Read our full guide to Train Street Hanoi →

How to experience it safely:

  • Find a café along the tracks, order a drink, and wait
  • Trains typically pass around late afternoon and evening (ask locals for exact times)
  • Follow every instruction from café owners — they know when it's safe

📅 Trains typically run at 4:00pm, 7:00pm, and 9:00pm, but schedules change. Ask a local café owner—they always know.

When the train passes, everything changes for a few seconds. Then life continues as if nothing happened. It's unforgettable.

📍 From MiaCasa Hanoi, it's a 5-minute walk. Many guests visit at 6am or 8pm to avoid crowds.

Evening

Take a Grab to Hồ Tây (West Lake) for sunset. Walk along the water, find a lakeside café, and enjoy Hanoi at its calmest.

Dinner: Try chả cá (turmeric fish with dill) or revisit a favorite street food spot.

Day 3: West Lake and a Slower Hanoi

By the third day, you'll want to slow down. That's exactly the right instinct.

Morning

  • Head to Hồ Tây (West Lake) — Hanoi's largest lake, surrounded by quiet neighborhoods
  • Visit Trấn Quốc Pagoda, the oldest Buddhist temple in Hanoi (1,500 years old)
  • Walk along the water or cycle the lakeside path
  • Stop at a café overlooking the lake — order cà phê trứng (egg coffee) if you haven't yet

Afternoon

  • Return to the city center at your own pace
  • Do some light shopping — look for silk, handicrafts, or local art
  • Revisit any streets or cafés that caught your attention earlier
  • Try any food you missed (bánh cuốn, bún riêu, or chè for dessert)

Evening

End your trip with a relaxed dinner — no rushing, no schedule. Just enjoy Hanoi one last time.

💡 Consider a local cooking class or water puppet show if time allows.

Final Thoughts

Three days in Hanoi is not about doing everything.

It's about finding a rhythm — between busy streets and quiet moments, between food and conversation, between chaos and calm.

That's what makes the city memorable.
