3 Days in Hanoi: A Complete Itinerary (2026)

The perfect 3-day Hanoi itinerary for first-time visitors — including where to eat, what to see, hidden cafés, Train Street, and where to stay.

  • Day 1: Old Quarter & Street Food

  • FAQs

Planning 3 days in Hanoi? This itinerary helps you experience the city beyond the usual tourist checklist—with local food, quieter neighborhoods, and places that make Hanoi memorable.

🌅 Day 1: Old Quarter, Street Food, and First Impressions

Start your trip in the heart of the chaotic, colorful historic center of Hanoi—the Old Quarter.

☀️ Morning

Begin at Hoàn Kiếm Lake (Lake of the Restored Sword) before the crowds arrive. Walk across the red Thê Húc Bridge to Ngọc Sơn Temple. Then wander into the maze of 36 ancient streets, each named after the trade once practiced there.

🍜 What to Eat Today

  • BREAKFAST Phở — find a busy street stall, not a restaurant
  • LUNCH Bánh mì — the perfect Vietnamese sandwich
  • DINNER Bún chả — grilled pork with noodles (what Obama ate with Bourdain)

🌙 Evening

Walk through the night market (weekends only) or find a rooftop café with lake views. The Old Quarter glows at night.

📚 Day 2: Culture, Cafés, and Train Street

Slow things down on your second day. This is when Hanoi starts to feel real.

☀️ Morning: Văn Miếu Area

Visit Văn Miếu – Quốc Tử Giám (Temple of Literature), Vietnam's first university — one of Hanoi's most peaceful sites. Afterward, walk through the surrounding streets. This area feels calmer, more residential, and genuinely local.

💡 Tip: MiaCasa Hanoi is located in this neighborhood — you can see what local life actually looks like.

🚂 Afternoon: Train Street

Head to one of Hanoi's most unique attractions — Train Street, where trains pass just inches from homes and cafés.

How to experience it safely:

  • Find a café along the tracks, order a drink, and wait
  • Trains typically pass around late afternoon and evening (ask locals for exact times)
  • Follow every instruction from café owners — they know when it's safe

📍 From MiaCasa Hanoi, it's a 5-minute walk. Many guests visit at 6am or 8pm to avoid crowds.

🌿 Day 3: West Lake and a Slower Hanoi

By the third day, you'll want to slow down. That's exactly the right instinct.

☀️ Morning

Head to Hồ Tây (West Lake) — Hanoi's largest lake, surrounded by quiet neighborhoods. Visit Trấn Quốc Pagoda, the oldest Buddhist temple in Hanoi (1,500 years old). Stop at a café overlooking the lake — order cà phê trứng (egg coffee) if you haven't yet.

🌙 Evening

End your trip with a relaxed dinner — no rushing, no schedule. Just enjoy Hanoi one last time.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is 3 days enough for Hanoi?

Yes, 3 days is enough to experience Hanoi's highlights including the Old Quarter, Hoàn Kiếm Lake, Train Street, Văn Miếu (Temple of Literature), and West Lake, plus enjoy the local food scene.

What is the best area to stay in Hanoi for 3 days?

The Old Quarter is best for first-time visitors who want to be in the center of food, nightlife, and attractions. For a quieter, more local experience, stay near Văn Miếu or Ba Đình areas — like MiaCasa Hanoi.

Is Train Street worth visiting?

Yes, Train Street is one of Hanoi's most unique attractions. Visit early morning or late evening to avoid crowds. Always follow safety instructions from local café owners.

What should I eat in Hanoi?

Must-try dishes include: Phở (noodle soup), Bún chả (grilled pork with noodles), Bánh mì (Vietnamese sandwich), and Cà phê trứng (egg coffee).
