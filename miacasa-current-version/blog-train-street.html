Train Street Hanoi: Full Guide (2026)

📅 April 22, 2026 · ☕ 6 min read · ✍️ By MiaCasa Team
The train passing through Train Street — just inches from local homes and cafés

Train Street is one of the most unusual places in Hanoi.

A narrow residential street. Homes just inches from the tracks. Cafés set up right beside them. And a train that passes through it all, just a few times each day.

It's not built for tourists—but that's exactly why people come.

What is Train Street?

Train Street is a stretch of railway running through a residential neighborhood in central Hanoi.

Locals live, cook, and run small cafés right next to the tracks. When the train approaches, everything shifts. Chairs are moved. People step back. Then the train passes—and life continues.

Is Train Street still open in 2026?

This is one of the most common questions.

Access to Train Street has been restricted at times for safety reasons. In practice, many visitors still enter through cafés or local access points. The situation can depend on:

  • Local conditions
  • Time of day
  • Whether you enter through a café

Train Street Hanoi Train Times (2026 Guide)

Train times are not fixed, and this is important to understand.

Trains usually pass several times a day, most often:

  • Morning: around 8:30 AM – 12:00 PM
  • Afternoon: around 3:00 PM – 4:30 PM
  • Evening: around 7:00 PM – 10:00 PM

You may also see specific times listed online, such as 4:10 PM, 7:20 PM, or 9:50 PM—but delays are common and schedules can change.

Unlike many guides that list exact times, Train Street operates on a flexible timetable.

👉 The best way to know the exact timing is simple: Ask a café owner when you arrive—they always have the most accurate information.

Where is Train Street?

Train Street is located near Hanoi Railway Station, not far from the Old Quarter.

The most visited sections are along:

  • Trần Phú Street
  • Phùng Hưng Street

How to visit Train Street safely

This is not a controlled tourist attraction—it's an active railway.

  • Always listen to café staff or locals
  • Stay clear of the tracks when told
  • Do not stand in the middle for photos
  • Avoid blocking the train
  • Choose a café instead of standing freely

If something feels unsafe, step back.

Best way to experience Train Street

The best way is simple.

Find a small café along the tracks. Sit down. Order a drink. Wait.

  • A safer experience
  • A better view
  • A more relaxed atmosphere

You'll also notice how the entire street prepares before the train arrives.

What does it feel like?

A few minutes before the train arrives, everything changes.

People move. Chairs disappear. The space clears.

Then suddenly, the train is there—loud, close, and surprisingly fast.

It passes in seconds.

Then just as quickly, the street returns to normal.

Best time to visit

🌅 Early morning

  • Fewer people
  • More local atmosphere

🌆 Evening

  • Livelier cafés
  • Better lighting and energy

Staying near Train Street

MiaCasa Hanoi is located in the Văn Miếu area, within walking distance of Train Street.

  • Visit early or late
  • Avoid crowds
  • Experience the area more naturally

Final thoughts

Train Street is not just about the train.

It's about the contrast—between daily life and something unexpected passing through it.

If you visit with respect and awareness, it can be one of the most unique experiences in Hanoi.
